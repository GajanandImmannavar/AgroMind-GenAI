Disease Name: Powdery Mildew

Crop:
Cherry (including sour cherry)

Scientific Name:
Podosphaera clandestina

Disease Type:
Fungal Disease

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, young shoots, and fruits

Description:
Powdery Mildew is a common disease that affects cherry trees. It usually appears during warm and humid weather. The disease forms a white powder-like layer on leaves and young shoots and can reduce plant growth and fruit quality if not controlled early.

Cause:
- Caused by a fungus called Podosphaera clandestina
- Spreads easily in warm and humid weather
- The fungus grows on the surface of leaves and shoots

Favorable Conditions:
- Warm temperatures
- High humidity
- Poor air circulation
- Crowded plant growth

Symptoms:
- White powder-like layer on leaves
- Leaves may curl or become distorted
- Young shoots may grow slowly
- Small white patches may appear on fruits
- Infected leaves may dry and fall early

Disease Spread:
- Spores spread through wind
- Infection spreads from infected leaves and shoots
- Humid conditions help the fungus grow

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
1. Remove infected leaves and shoots.
2. Prune branches to improve air circulation.
3. Keep the orchard clean.
4. Spray recommended fungicide early.
5. Check plants regularly for early signs.

Recommended Medicine:
- Sulfur fungicide
- Potassium bicarbonate spray
- Myclobutanil

How to Apply:
- Spray fungicide evenly on leaves and shoots.
- Apply at the early stage of infection.
- Repeat spray every 7–10 days if needed.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fungicide cost: ₹800 – ₹1800 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1100 – ₹2500 per acre

Recovery Time:
Plants may improve in about 1–2 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
- Maintain good air circulation between trees.
- Avoid overcrowding of plants.
- Prune trees regularly.
- Remove infected plant parts quickly.
- Monitor plants regularly.

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular pruning
- Orchard cleanliness
- Balanced fertilizer use
- Proper spacing between trees

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, the disease can reduce fruit quality and plant growth.

Farmer Advice:
Check leaves regularly for white powder-like spots. Early spraying and proper pruning can help control the disease.
