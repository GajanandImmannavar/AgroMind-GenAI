Disease Name: Target Spot

Crop:
Tomato

Scientific Name:
Corynespora cassiicola

Disease Type:
Fungal Disease

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, and fruits

Description:
Target Spot is a disease that affects tomato plants. It spreads mainly in warm and humid conditions. The disease causes brown spots with ring-like patterns on leaves and can damage fruits if not controlled early.

Cause:
- Caused by a fungus called Corynespora cassiicola
- Spreads in warm and humid weather
- The fungus survives in infected plant debris in the field

Favorable Conditions:
- Warm temperatures
- High humidity
- Frequent rainfall
- Poor air circulation between plants

Symptoms:
- Brown spots with ring pattern on leaves
- Yellow area around spots
- Leaves may dry and fall
- Fruits may show dark patches
- Severe infection weakens the plant

Disease Spread:
- Spores spread through wind
- Rain splash spreads the fungus
- Infected plant debris in the soil spreads the disease

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
1. Remove infected leaves and plant parts.
2. Spray recommended fungicide early.
3. Maintain proper spacing between plants.
4. Improve air circulation in the field.
5. Monitor plants regularly.

Recommended Medicine:
- Mancozeb
- Chlorothalonil
- Copper-based fungicides

How to Apply:
- Spray fungicide evenly on leaves and fruits.
- Apply when early symptoms appear.
- Repeat spraying every 7–10 days if needed.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fungicide cost: ₹800 – ₹1800 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1100 – ₹2500 per acre

Recovery Time:
Plants may improve in about 1–2 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
- Avoid overcrowding plants
- Maintain proper spacing
- Remove infected plant parts
- Keep field clean

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular field inspection
- Balanced fertilizer use
- Proper plant spacing
- Good field sanitation

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, Target Spot can reduce tomato plant health and crop yield.

Farmer Advice:
Check tomato leaves regularly for brown spots with ring patterns. Early fungicide spraying and proper field management help control the disease.
