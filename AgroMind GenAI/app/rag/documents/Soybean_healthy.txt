Disease Name: Healthy Plant

Crop:
Soybean

Scientific Name:
Glycine max

Plant Condition:
Healthy (No disease)

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, and pods are healthy

Description:
The soybean plant is healthy and growing normally. The leaves are green and fresh, and the plant shows strong growth. Proper farming practices help maintain crop health and improve soybean production.

Cause:
- No disease detected
- Proper crop care
- Good growing conditions

Favorable Conditions:
- Adequate sunlight
- Healthy soil with nutrients
- Proper irrigation
- Good air circulation

Symptoms:
- Green and healthy leaves
- No unusual spots or yellowing
- Strong plant growth
- Normal pod development

Plant Condition Spread:
- Not applicable because the plant is healthy

Steps to Keep Plant Healthy:
1. Maintain proper irrigation based on crop needs.
2. Apply balanced fertilizers for good plant growth.
3. Remove weeds from the field.
4. Monitor crops regularly for early signs of disease or pests.
5. Maintain field cleanliness.

Recommended Nutrients / Fertilizer:
- Organic compost
- NPK balanced fertilizer
- Farmyard manure

How to Apply:
- Apply fertilizer near the root area.
- Water plants after fertilizer application.
- Follow recommended fertilizer schedule.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fertilizer cost: ₹1000 – ₹3000 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1300 – ₹3700 per acre

Plant Monitoring Time:
Check crop condition every 7–10 days.

Prevention:
- Maintain proper irrigation
- Use quality seeds
- Keep field clean
- Maintain proper spacing between plants

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular field inspection
- Balanced fertilizer use
- Proper soil management
- Good weed control

Effect on Yield:
Healthy plants produce better quality soybeans and higher yield.

Farmer Advice:
Continue good farming practices such as proper watering, fertilization, and regular monitoring to keep soybean plants healthy.
