Disease Name: Cedar Apple Rust

Crop:
Apple

Scientific Name:
Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae

Disease Type:
Fungal Disease

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves and fruits

Description:
Cedar Apple Rust is a disease that affects apple trees. It usually spreads during the rainy season. The fungus needs two plants to live: apple trees and cedar or juniper trees. The disease spreads from cedar trees to apple trees through wind during wet weather.

Cause:
- Caused by a fungus called Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae
- Spreads during rainy and humid weather
- Often spreads from nearby cedar or juniper trees

Favorable Conditions:
- Rainy season
- Cool and moist weather
- Presence of nearby cedar or juniper trees
- Wet leaves for long time

Symptoms:
- Yellow or orange spots on leaves
- Bright orange patches on the leaf surface
- Leaves may fall early
- Small spots may appear on fruits
- In severe cases, fruit quality may decrease

Disease Spread:
- Spores travel through wind
- Rain and moisture help the fungus grow
- Infection often comes from nearby cedar or juniper trees

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
1. Remove infected leaves from the tree.
2. Spray recommended fungicide early.
3. Keep the orchard clean.
4. Prune branches to improve air flow.
5. Check plants regularly for early symptoms.

Recommended Medicine:
- Mancozeb
- Myclobutanil
- Captan
- Sulfur spray

How to Apply:
- Spray fungicide on leaves and fruits.
- Start spraying when the first symptoms appear.
- Repeat spraying every 7–10 days if needed.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fungicide cost: ₹900 – ₹2000
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1200 – ₹2700 per acre

Recovery Time:
Plants may improve in about 1–3 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
- Remove nearby infected cedar or juniper plants if possible.
- Maintain proper distance between apple trees.
- Keep good air circulation in the orchard.
- Remove infected plant parts quickly.

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular pruning of trees
- Maintain orchard cleanliness
- Monitor plants often for disease
- Use balanced fertilizer

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, the disease can reduce fruit quality and yield.

Farmer Advice:
Check leaves regularly for yellow or orange spots. Early fungicide spray and good orchard care can help control the disease.
