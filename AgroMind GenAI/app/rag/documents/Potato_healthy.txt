Disease Name: Healthy Plant

Crop:
Potato

Scientific Name:
Solanum tuberosum

Plant Condition:
Healthy (No disease)

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, and tubers are healthy

Description:
The potato plant is healthy and growing normally. The leaves are green and fresh, and the tubers develop properly in the soil. Good farming practices help maintain plant health and improve potato yield.

Cause:
- No disease detected
- Proper plant care
- Good growing conditions

Favorable Conditions:
- Adequate sunlight
- Healthy soil with nutrients
- Proper irrigation
- Good air circulation

Symptoms:
- Leaves are green and healthy
- No spots or discoloration
- Strong plant growth
- Good tuber development in the soil

Plant Condition Spread:
- Not applicable because the plant is healthy

Steps to Keep Plant Healthy:
1. Maintain proper watering based on crop needs.
2. Apply balanced fertilizers for healthy growth.
3. Remove weeds from the field.
4. Monitor plants regularly for early signs of disease or pests.
5. Keep the field clean.

Recommended Nutrients / Fertilizer:
- Organic compost
- NPK balanced fertilizer
- Farmyard manure

How to Apply:
- Apply fertilizer near the root area.
- Water the plants after fertilizer application.
- Follow recommended fertilizer schedule.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fertilizer cost: ₹1000 – ₹3000 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1300 – ₹3700 per acre

Plant Monitoring Time:
Check plant condition every 7–10 days.

Prevention:
- Maintain proper watering
- Use healthy seed potatoes
- Keep field clean
- Maintain proper spacing between plants

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular field inspection
- Balanced fertilizer use
- Good soil management
- Proper weed control

Effect on Yield:
Healthy plants produce good quality potatoes and higher yield.

Farmer Advice:
Continue good farming practices such as proper watering, fertilization, and regular monitoring to keep potato plants healthy.
