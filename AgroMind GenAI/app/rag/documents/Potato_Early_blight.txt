Disease Name: Early Blight

Crop:
Potato

Scientific Name:
Alternaria solani

Disease Type:
Fungal Disease

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, and sometimes tubers

Description:
Early Blight is a common disease in potato plants. It spreads mainly in warm and humid weather. The disease causes brown circular spots on leaves and can weaken the plant if not treated early.

Cause:
- Caused by a fungus called Alternaria solani
- Spreads in warm and humid weather
- The fungus survives in infected plant debris in the field

Favorable Conditions:
- Warm temperatures
- High humidity
- Frequent rain
- Poor air circulation between plants

Symptoms:
- Brown circular spots on leaves
- Yellow area around the spots
- Spots may grow larger over time
- Leaves may dry and fall early
- Plant growth may become weak

Disease Spread:
- Spores spread through wind
- Rain splash spreads the fungus
- Infected plant debris in the soil

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
1. Remove infected leaves from the plants.
2. Spray recommended fungicide early.
3. Maintain proper spacing between plants.
4. Keep the field clean.
5. Monitor crops regularly.

Recommended Medicine:
- Mancozeb
- Chlorothalonil
- Copper-based fungicides

How to Apply:
- Spray fungicide evenly on leaves and stems.
- Apply when early symptoms appear.
- Repeat spraying every 7–10 days if necessary.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fungicide cost: ₹800 – ₹1800 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1100 – ₹2500 per acre

Recovery Time:
Plants may improve in about 1–2 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
- Use healthy and certified seeds
- Avoid excess watering
- Practice crop rotation
- Keep field clean

Best Farming Practices:
- Maintain proper plant spacing
- Remove plant debris after harvest
- Regular crop monitoring
- Use balanced fertilizers

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, Early Blight can reduce potato plant health and crop yield.

Farmer Advice:
Check potato leaves regularly for brown circular spots with yellow edges. Early fungicide spraying and good field management help control the disease.
