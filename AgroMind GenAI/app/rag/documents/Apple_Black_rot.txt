Disease Name: Apple Black Rot

Crop: Apple

Scientific Name: Botryosphaeria obtusa

Disease Type: Fungal Disease

Affected Plant Parts: Leaves, fruits, branches

Description:
Apple Black Rot is a disease that affects apple trees. It spreads mostly in warm and wet weather and damages leaves and fruits.

Cause:
Caused by a fungus called Botryosphaeria obtusa.

Favorable Conditions:
Warm weather, high humidity, and rainy conditions.

Symptoms:
Brown or black spots on leaves.
Dark rotten patches on fruits.
Fruits may shrink and dry.

Disease Spread:
Spreads through wind, rain, and infected plant parts.

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
Remove infected leaves and fruits.
Prune infected branches.
Spray recommended fungicide.

Recommended Medicine:
Mancozeb
Captan
Copper fungicide

How to Apply:
Spray evenly on leaves and fruits every 7–10 days.

Cost per Acre:
₹1200 – ₹2700 per acre.

Recovery Time:
1–3 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
Keep orchard clean.
Remove fallen leaves.
Improve air flow between trees.

Best Farming Practices:
Regular pruning
Orchard cleaning
Balanced fertilizer use

Effect on Yield:
Can reduce fruit quality and production.

Farmer Advice:
Check trees regularly and remove infected plant parts quickly.
