Disease Name: Late Blight

Crop:
Tomato

Scientific Name:
Phytophthora infestans

Disease Type:
Fungal-like Disease (Oomycete)

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, and fruits

Description:
Late Blight is a serious disease in tomato plants. It spreads quickly in cool and wet weather. The disease causes large dark patches on leaves and can damage fruits if not controlled early.

Cause:
- Caused by Phytophthora infestans
- Spreads quickly in cool and wet weather
- Rain, wind, and infected plant parts help spread the disease

Favorable Conditions:
- Cool temperatures
- High humidity
- Frequent rainfall
- Wet leaves for long periods

Symptoms:
- Large dark brown or black patches on leaves
- White growth under leaves in humid weather
- Leaves may rot and dry quickly
- Fruits may develop brown rotten areas
- Severe infection can destroy plants quickly

Disease Spread:
- Spores spread through wind and rain
- Infected seedlings can spread the disease
- Wet field conditions increase infection

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
1. Remove infected plants immediately.
2. Spray recommended fungicide early.
3. Improve drainage to reduce excess moisture.
4. Maintain proper spacing between plants.
5. Monitor crops daily.

Recommended Medicine:
- Mancozeb
- Chlorothalonil
- Copper-based fungicides

How to Apply:
- Spray fungicide evenly on leaves and stems.
- Apply when early symptoms appear.
- Repeat spraying every 7–10 days during wet weather.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fungicide cost: ₹900 – ₹2000 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1200 – ₹2700 per acre

Recovery Time:
Plants may improve in about 1–2 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
- Avoid water staying on leaves
- Improve drainage in the field
- Use healthy seedlings
- Remove infected plants immediately

Best Farming Practices:
- Maintain proper plant spacing
- Improve field drainage
- Regular crop monitoring
- Use balanced fertilizers

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, Late Blight can destroy large parts of the tomato crop and greatly reduce yield.

Farmer Advice:
Check tomato leaves regularly for large dark patches and white growth under leaves. Early fungicide spraying and proper field drainage help control the disease.
