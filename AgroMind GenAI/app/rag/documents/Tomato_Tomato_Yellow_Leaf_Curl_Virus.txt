Disease Name: Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Crop:
Tomato

Scientific Name:
Tomato yellow leaf curl virus (TYLCV)

Disease Type:
Viral Disease

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, and fruits

Description:
Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus is a serious disease that affects tomato plants. It spreads mainly through small insects called whiteflies. The virus causes yellowing and curling of leaves and reduces plant growth and fruit production.

Cause:
- Caused by Tomato yellow leaf curl virus (TYLCV)
- Spread by whitefly insects (Bemisia tabaci)
- Spreads faster in warm weather

Favorable Conditions:
- Warm temperatures
- High whitefly population
- Poor pest control
- Continuous tomato cultivation

Symptoms:
- Yellowing of leaves
- Leaves curl upward
- Leaves become smaller and thicker
- Stunted plant growth
- Reduced fruit production

Disease Spread:
- Spread by whitefly insects feeding on plants
- Whiteflies carry the virus from infected plants
- Infection spreads quickly in fields with high whitefly population

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
1. Remove infected plants immediately.
2. Control whitefly insects using recommended insecticides.
3. Use insect-proof nets in nursery.
4. Maintain proper field hygiene.
5. Monitor plants regularly for early symptoms.

Recommended Medicine (Whitefly Control):
- Imidacloprid
- Thiamethoxam
- Spinosad
- Neem oil spray

How to Apply:
- Spray insecticide evenly on leaves, especially the underside.
- Apply when whiteflies are first noticed.
- Repeat spraying based on pest presence.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Insecticide cost: ₹1000 – ₹2200 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1300 – ₹2900 per acre

Recovery Time:
Infected plants cannot recover. Early removal helps protect healthy plants.

Prevention:
- Control whitefly population
- Use resistant tomato varieties
- Remove infected plants early
- Use insect-proof nets in nursery

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular pest monitoring
- Maintain field cleanliness
- Use balanced fertilizers
- Proper crop rotation

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus can greatly reduce tomato yield and plant growth.

Farmer Advice:
Check tomato plants regularly for yellowing and upward curling leaves. Control whiteflies early and remove infected plants to prevent the virus from spreading.
