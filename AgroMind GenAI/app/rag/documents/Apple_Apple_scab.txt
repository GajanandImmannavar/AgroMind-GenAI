Disease Name: Apple Scab

Scientific Name:
Venturia inaequalis

Disease Type:
Fungal Disease

Affected Crop:
Apple

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves and fruits

Description:
Apple Scab is a common disease in apple trees. It spreads mostly in cool and wet weather. The disease mainly affects leaves and fruits and can reduce the quality of apples if not controlled early.

Cause:
This disease is caused by a fungus called Venturia inaequalis. The fungus lives in infected fallen leaves and spreads through wind and rain.

Favorable Conditions:
- Cool weather
- High moisture or humidity
- Frequent rain
- Wet leaves for long time

Symptoms:
- Dark green or brown spots on leaves
- Leaves may dry and fall early
- Rough dark spots on fruits
- Fruits may become cracked or deformed
- Poor fruit quality

How the Disease Spreads:
- Wind
- Rain splash
- Infected fallen leaves in the field
- Poor cleaning of the orchard

Steps to Make Plant Healthy (Treatment):
1. Remove infected leaves and fruits from the tree.
2. Clean fallen leaves from the ground.
3. Spray recommended fungicide on the plants.
4. Prune branches to allow better air flow.
5. Check plants regularly for early signs.

Recommended Fungicides:
- Mancozeb
- Captan
- Sulfur spray

How to Apply:
- Spray the medicine on leaves and fruits.
- Spray in the early stage of disease.
- Repeat spraying every 7–10 days if needed.

Cost of Treatment (Approximate per Acre):
- Fungicide cost: ₹800 – ₹2000 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: around ₹1100 – ₹2700 per acre

Recovery Time:
Plants may improve in about 1–3 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
- Grow disease-resistant apple varieties.
- Avoid watering leaves from above.
- Keep proper distance between trees.
- Clean the orchard regularly.
- Remove infected plant parts.

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular pruning of trees
- Clean the orchard area
- Check plants often for disease
- Use balanced fertilizer

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, it can reduce apple production by 50–70%.

Farmer Advice:
Check plants regularly for spots on leaves. Remove infected leaves quickly and spray fungicide early to stop the disease from spreading.
