Disease Name: Healthy Plant

Crop:
Tomato

Scientific Name:
Solanum lycopersicum

Plant Condition:
Healthy (No disease)

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, flowers, and fruits are healthy

Description:
The tomato plant is healthy and growing normally. The leaves are green and fresh, and the plant produces flowers and fruits normally. Good farming practices help maintain plant health and improve crop production.

Cause:
- No disease detected
- Proper plant care
- Good growing conditions

Favorable Conditions:
- Adequate sunlight
- Healthy soil with nutrients
- Proper irrigation
- Good air circulation

Symptoms:
- Green and fresh leaves
- No unusual spots or discoloration
- Strong plant growth
- Normal flowering and fruiting

Plant Condition Spread:
- Not applicable because the plant is healthy

Steps to Keep Plant Healthy:
1. Maintain regular watering based on crop needs.
2. Apply balanced fertilizers for good plant growth.
3. Remove weeds from the field.
4. Monitor plants regularly for early signs of disease or pests.
5. Keep the field clean.

Recommended Nutrients / Fertilizer:
- Organic compost
- NPK balanced fertilizer
- Farmyard manure

How to Apply:
- Apply fertilizer near the root area.
- Water plants after fertilizer application.
- Follow recommended fertilizer schedule.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Fertilizer cost: ₹1000 – ₹3000 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1300 – ₹3700 per acre

Plant Monitoring Time:
Check plant condition every 7–10 days.

Prevention:
- Maintain proper watering
- Use healthy seeds or seedlings
- Keep field clean
- Maintain proper spacing between plants

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular field inspection
- Balanced fertilizer use
- Proper soil management
- Good weed control

Effect on Yield:
Healthy plants produce better quality tomatoes and higher yield.

Farmer Advice:
Continue good farming practices such as proper watering, fertilization, and regular monitoring to keep tomato plants healthy.
