Disease Name: Bacterial Spot

Crop:
Bell Pepper

Scientific Name:
Xanthomonas campestris pv. vesicatoria

Disease Type:
Bacterial Disease

Affected Plant Parts:
Leaves, stems, and fruits

Description:
Bacterial Spot is a common disease in bell pepper plants. It spreads mostly in warm and wet weather. The disease causes dark spots on leaves and fruits and can reduce crop quality if not controlled early.

Cause:
- Caused by a bacterium called Xanthomonas campestris pv. vesicatoria
- Spreads in warm and humid weather
- Rain splash and wind help spread the bacteria

Favorable Conditions:
- Warm temperatures
- High humidity
- Frequent rainfall
- Wet leaves for long periods

Symptoms:
- Small dark or black spots on leaves
- Yellow areas around the spots
- Leaves may turn yellow and fall early
- Rough dark spots on fruits
- Reduced fruit quality

Disease Spread:
- Spread through rain splash
- Wind can carry bacteria
- Infected seeds and plant material can spread the disease

Steps to Make Plant Healthy:
1. Remove infected leaves and fruits from the plant.
2. Keep the field clean and remove plant debris.
3. Spray recommended bactericide early.
4. Maintain proper spacing between plants.
5. Monitor plants regularly.

Recommended Medicine:
- Copper oxychloride
- Copper hydroxide
- Streptomycin spray (where recommended)

How to Apply:
- Spray bactericide evenly on leaves and fruits.
- Apply when early symptoms appear.
- Repeat spraying every 7–10 days if necessary.

Cost per Acre (Approximate):
- Bactericide cost: ₹900 – ₹1800 per acre
- Labor cost: ₹300 – ₹700
- Total cost: about ₹1200 – ₹2500 per acre

Recovery Time:
Plants may improve in about 1–2 weeks if treated early.

Prevention:
- Use disease-free seeds
- Avoid working in wet fields
- Maintain proper spacing between plants
- Keep tools and equipment clean

Best Farming Practices:
- Regular field inspection
- Balanced fertilizer use
- Good soil drainage
- Proper weed control

Effect on Yield:
If not controlled, Bacterial Spot can reduce pepper yield and fruit quality.

Farmer Advice:
Check pepper leaves and fruits regularly for dark spots. Early bactericide spraying and good field hygiene help control the disease.
